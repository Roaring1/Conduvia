# What is Conduvia?

Conduvia is a survival-and-industry game with one audacious promise: **you rebuild
the modern world from a pile of sticks, and the recipes are real.**

Not “game-logic real.” Actually real. To get steel you make coke and run a blast
furnace. To get aluminium you run electrolysis. To get a microchip you grow a
silicon crystal and slice it into wafers. The fun isn’t memorising a crafting
tree someone invented — it’s discovering that **you already know how the world
works**, and now you get to build it with your hands.

## The loop

1. **Gather** what’s around you — plants, stone, wood, water.
2. **Craft** at a station that mirrors a real tool: a kiln, a bloomery, an arc
   furnace, a Czochralski puller.
3. **Unlock** the next tier, which gives you a new *capability* — controlled
   heat, then metal, then power, then chemistry, then electronics.
4. **Climb** until you can build a rocket and launch it to orbit. That’s the win.

## Why the order can’t be skipped

The tech tree isn’t gated by arbitrary level locks — it’s gated by **physics**.
You can’t smelt aluminium over a campfire because it takes industrial
electricity. You can’t make that electricity at scale without steel machines.
You can’t make steel without coke-grade heat. Each rung literally stands on the
one below it. That’s why the climb feels earned.

!!! note "~85% honest"
    Conduvia compresses time and fudges a few quantities so a session is fun, but
    the *processes* are faithful. Where something is simplified, the guide says so.
