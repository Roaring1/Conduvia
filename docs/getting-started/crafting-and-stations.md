# Crafting & stations

## Hand-crafting vs stations

Simple things you can make anywhere from your inventory. Anything that needs
**heat, force, or process** needs a **station** — a placed machine modeled on a
real piece of equipment. You craft *at* a station by putting inputs in and
collecting outputs.

## Reading a recipe

Hover any item and press ++r++ (**Recipes**) to see exactly how it’s made:
what goes **in**, what comes **out**, which **station** does the work, and how
long it takes. Press ++u++ (**Uses**) to go the other way — what an item feeds
into. Between those two keys you can trace the whole tree without leaving the game.

In this manual, recipes and tasks show items as chips like :ci[rope|2] (an item
plus a count). On the [item pages](../items/index.md), each entry lists what it’s
for and which station builds it.

## How a station works

Most stations have up to three kinds of slots:

- <span class="slot in">input</span> — raw materials go here
- <span class="slot fuel">fuel</span> — if the process needs heat (charcoal,
  coke, etc.)
- <span class="slot out">output</span> — finished goods land here

Some stations have **no panel** at all — they do their job just by being placed
(markers, info posts, sleeping spots). The [Stations reference](../stations/index.md)
lists every station with its slots and what it’s for.

## The pattern that repeats forever

Every tier is the same shape at a higher temperature:

> **get a heat/energy source → build the station that uses it → process raw
> material into a refined material → use that material to build the next, hotter
> station.**

Campfire → kiln → bloomery → blast furnace → arc furnace → crystal puller.
Once you see the pattern, you can predict what the game wants next.
