<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### The Metal Ages

You have fire, clay, and a kiln that reaches over 1,000 degrees.
You have everything you need to work metal.

Three metals. Three ages. Each one harder to make and more useful than the last.

- **Copper**: hammer it cold, then learn to melt it
- **Bronze**: find tin, smelt it, alloy it with copper
- **Iron**: gather bog iron, build a bloomery, hammer out wrought iron

The Steam chapter will ask you for a lot of iron.
This is where you learn to make it.



<div class="qb-hint" markdown>
**Tip** · **Use the recipe browser throughout this chapter.**  Quest tasks show *what* to produce but not the exact ingredients.  Hover any item and press **R** to see its recipe, or press **U** to see what uses it.  Every process in this chapter is discoverable that way -- you don't need to guess.
</div>





<div class="qb-hint" markdown>
**Tip** · **Both paths reward you equally.** You must complete the Bronze path (bronze pickaxe) AND the Iron path (iron hammer) to reach the checkpoint.  Both tools unlock Tier 2 ore mining -- coal, hematite, cassiterite in harder deposits.  You will need them for the Steam chapter.
</div>
</div>

### What changes here

You stop finding materials and start producing them.
That distinction -- found vs. produced -- is the conceptual leap behind all of metallurgy.
</details>

<h3 class="quest-card__title">Cold-Hammered Copper</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>The Metal Ages</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Cold-hammered copper

Native copper doesn't need a furnace. It needs a hammerstone and patience.

Cold hammering work-hardens copper -- each strike makes it stronger,
but eventually it becomes brittle. Anneal it in your campfire to
restore workability, then hammer again.

This is the Copper Age. It predates smelting by thousands of years.
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-retrieve">retrieve</span> Mine native copper (already visible -- no survey needed).
    <br>yields: :ci[raw_ore_native_copper|20]
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Hammer native copper into workable nuggets at a stone anvil.
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[copper_nugget_hammered|4] :ci[raw_ore_native_copper|10]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### Annealing

Heating metal and letting it cool slowly was discovered before smelting.
Copperworkers noticed that fire softened their work-hardened pieces.
This observation -- that heat changes metal -- is the conceptual leap
that eventually leads to all of metallurgy.
</details>
</section>

<h3 class="quest-card__title">Crucible Smelting</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Cold-Hammered Copper</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Crucible smelting

Your kiln can reach 1,100 °C. Copper melts at 1,085 °C.

You already have everything you need.

Build a clay crucible. Load it with native copper. Fire it in the kiln.
Pour the liquid copper into a stone mold.
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Craft clay crucibles for holding a metal charge.
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Carve a stone ingot mold.
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Smelt native copper in the crucible and cast copper ingots.
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[copper_ingot|4] :ci[clay_crucible_small|1]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### This is the moment your game changes

You can now produce a material that is *liquid* and can be *shaped*.
From here, every metal follows the same logic: heat, reduce, cast.
The only variables are temperature requirements and reduction chemistry.
</details>
</section>

<h3 class="quest-card__title">Copper Tools</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Crucible Smelting</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Copper tools

Cast copper is soft but workable. Shape it into simple tools --
awls, chisels, small blades.

Copper tools wear quickly compared to stone, but they can be
re-cast when they break. Stone can't.

That's the real advantage: repairability.
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-retrieve">retrieve</span> Stockpile copper ingots for toolmaking and alloying.
    <br>yields: :ci[copper_ingot|6]
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[copper_ingot|6] :ci[stone_mold_ingot|1]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### Why copper matters

Copper is not better than stone for cutting.
It's better than stone for *remaking*.

A broken flint blade is waste. A broken copper awl goes back in the crucible.
</details>
</section>

<h3 class="quest-card__title">Find the Tin</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Copper Tools</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Find the tin

Bronze isn't copper. It's copper + tin.

Tin comes from cassiterite -- a heavy, dark pebble found in stream gravels.
It looks like nothing. It changes everything.

Look for dark, dense pebbles in stream deposits.
Cassiterite is visible to the naked eye if you know what you're looking for.
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-retrieve">retrieve</span> Collect cassiterite ore from stream gravels.
    <br>yields: :ci[raw_ore_cassiterite|12]
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[raw_ore_cassiterite|8]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### Why tin matters

Tin occurs in relatively few places, unlike common copper.
Bronze Age "globalization" happened because of this single alloy requirement.
Trade routes formed specifically to supply tin to copper-producing regions.
</details>
</section>

<header class="quest-card__head">
<span class="qbadge qbadge-main">Win-path</span>
<h3 class="quest-card__title">Smelt Tin</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Find the Tin</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Smelt tin

Tin melts at 232 °C -- well within campfire range, far below your kiln's capacity.

Load cassiterite into a crucible with charcoal. The carbon reduces the tin oxide
to metallic tin. Pour it into your stone mold.

Tin by itself is too soft for tools. But mixed with copper...
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Smelt cassiterite into tin ingots in the crucible.
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[tin_ingot|4]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### The ratio

Bronze is approximately 90% copper, 10% tin.
Too much tin makes it brittle. Too little and you haven't improved on pure copper.
</details>
</section>

<h3 class="quest-card__title">Cast Bronze</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Smelt Tin</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Cast bronze

Melt copper and tin together in a crucible.
The ratio matters: roughly 5 copper to 1 tin for gameplay.

Pour into molds. Cool. You have bronze.

Bronze is harder than copper, holds an edge better, and casts more precisely.
It's the first *engineered* material -- an alloy designed for performance.
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Alloy copper and tin to produce bronze ingots.
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Forge a bronze pickaxe (unlocks Tier 2 mining).
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[bronze_ingot|4] :ci[bronze_pickaxe|1]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### What bronze unlocks

The bronze pickaxe unlocks Tier 2 ore mining --
hematite, magnetite, and sulfide ores that stone and copper tools can't touch.

You now have a reason to go deeper.
</details>
</section>

<h3 class="quest-card__title">Bog Iron</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Copper Tools</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Bog iron

Bog iron forms in wetland areas where iron-bearing groundwater meets organic acids.

It's impure, spongy, and weak compared to later iron.
But it's everywhere. A village smith can collect it without mining equipment.

Look for orange-brown precipitate at the margins of standing water.
Bog iron is auto-surveyed -- you can see it without a geology hammer.
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-retrieve">retrieve</span> Gather bog iron from wetland deposits.
    <br>yields: :ci[raw_ore_bog_iron|30]
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[raw_ore_bog_iron|20]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### Why iron matters

Iron broke the palace monopolies on metallurgy that bronze had created.
Before bog iron, having metal tools required either living near a mine
or being part of a trade network.
After bog iron, any community with a wetland could make iron.
</details>
</section>

<h3 class="quest-card__title">Roasting Pit</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Bog Iron</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Roasting pit

Raw bog iron is full of moisture and volatile impurities.
Roasting drives these off before the bloomery step.

Build a simple roasting pit -- a shallow fire bed with ore layered on top.
The heat doesn't melt anything. It just dries and pre-treats the ore
so the bloomery can reduce it more efficiently.
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-retrieve">retrieve</span> Build and place a roasting pit.
    <br>yields: :ci[roasting_pit|1]
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Roast bog iron to prepare it for the bloomery.
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[roasted_ore_bog_iron|6]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### Not optional

Without roasting, your bloomery will consume more charcoal
and produce a smaller, slag-heavy bloom.
Roasting is where "I found some ore" becomes "I prepared a charge."
</details>
</section>

<header class="quest-card__head">
<span class="qbadge qbadge-main">Win-path</span>
<h3 class="quest-card__title">Build a Bloomery</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Roasting Pit</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Build a bloomery

A bloomery is a shaft furnace -- not a kiln.

The key difference: you force air through the base using bellows.
This gets the temperature high enough to reduce iron oxide to metallic iron,
even though it's not hot enough to melt iron.

Build the shaft from clay and stone. Add a tuyere (air inlet).
Connect bellows. Prepare charcoal.
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-retrieve">retrieve</span> Build and place a clay bloomery furnace.
    <br>yields: :ci[clay_bloomery_furnace|1]
- <span class="task-kind task-retrieve">retrieve</span> Have bellows ready for forced air.
    <br>yields: :ci[bellows|1]
- <span class="task-kind task-retrieve">retrieve</span> Stock charcoal for the bloomery charge.
    <br>yields: :ci[charcoal|20]
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[charcoal|20]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### What the bloomery does

Carbon monoxide from burning charcoal strips oxygen from the iron ore.
The iron collects as a spongy mass called a bloom.
It never melts. It just... consolidates.
</details>
</section>

<section class="quest-card qtype-main" markdown>
<header class="quest-card__head">
<span class="qbadge qbadge-main">Win-path</span>
<h3 class="quest-card__title">Extract the Bloom</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Build a Bloomery</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Extract the bloom

Charge the bloomery: roasted ore and charcoal in alternating layers.
Work the bellows. The reduction reaction takes time.

The result is a spongy mass of iron and slag -- a bloom.
It is NOT liquid iron. It never melts. It just consolidates.

Pull the bloom out while it's still red-hot. Work fast.
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Run the bloomery and extract iron blooms.
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[iron_bloom|2]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### The bloom isn't finished iron

It's iron contaminated with slag. The hammering is what removes the slag
and aligns the iron grain structure. Without hammering, the bloom is useless.
</details>
</section>

<section class="quest-card qtype-main" markdown>
<header class="quest-card__head">
<span class="qbadge qbadge-main">Win-path</span>
<h3 class="quest-card__title">Forge Wrought Iron</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Extract the Bloom</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### Forge wrought iron

The bloom needs to be worked.

Reheat it to orange-red. Strike it with a heavy hammer.
The slag squirts out as sparks and scale. Fold it. Reheat. Repeat.

This process is called consolidation. What you're making is wrought iron --
the same material every pre-industrial metalwork is built from.
</div>
<div class="quest-tasks" markdown>
**Do this:**
- <span class="task-kind task-retrieve">retrieve</span> Build and place a forge hearth.
    <br>yields: :ci[forge_hearth|1]
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Hammer blooms into wrought iron bars at the forge.
- <span class="task-kind task-produce">produce</span> Forge an iron hammer head and haft it.
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[wrought_iron_bar|4] :ci[tool_iron_hammer|1]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### The payoff

The iron hammer unlocks Tier 2 mining alongside the bronze pickaxe.
Both the Bronze and Iron paths open the same deeper ores --
historically accurate, and it gives you two routes to the Steam chapter.
</details>
</section>

<section class="quest-card qtype-checkpoint" markdown>

<span class="qbadge qbadge-checkpoint">Checkpoint</span>
<h3 class="quest-card__title">The Metal Ages Complete</h3>
</header>
<p class="quest-pre">Unlocks after: <em>Cast Bronze, Forge Wrought Iron</em></p>
<div class="quest-summary" markdown>
### The Metal Ages complete

**Both paths required. Bronze AND iron. Check your quest map -- one of them isn't done.**

You now understand metal.

Not as a found object -- as a produced material.

Copper from native deposits. Bronze from two ores combined in a specific ratio.
Iron from a reduction furnace that took all day to run.
Each one is harder to make and more useful than the last.

The Steam chapter is going to ask you for a lot of iron.
You know how to make it.
</div>
<p class="quest-rewards">Rewards: :ci[lootbag_tier_II|1]</p>
<details class="quest-lore" markdown>
<summary>Background</summary>

### What you've learned

- Metal can be shaped by hammering, melting, or both
- Alloying (bronze) creates materials better than either component
- Reduction (bloomery) extracts metal from ore using carbon chemistry
- Every metal process is: ore + heat + chemistry = metal + waste

The Steam tier builds on all of this.
</details>
</section>
