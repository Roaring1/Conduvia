# Card & badge legend

A quick key to the symbols used throughout this manual.

## Quest badges

| Badge | Meaning |
|---|---|
| <span class="qbadge qbadge-main">Win-path</span> | On the critical line to orbit. Do these. |
| <span class="qbadge qbadge-checkpoint">Checkpoint</span> | Closes out a chapter / unlocks the next tier. |
| <span class="qbadge qbadge-side">Branch</span> | Optional. Quality-of-life, comfort, or a side-system. |
| <span class="qbadge qbadge-info">Briefing</span> | Read-only intro or explainer, no crafting. |
| <span class="qbadge qbadge-exchange">Exchange</span> | Token trade — upgrades and bundles. |

## Quest card anatomy

Each quest card shows, top to bottom:

- the **badge** and **title**;
- **Unlocks after** — the quests you must finish first;
- a short **“what do I do”** summary in the game’s own voice;
- **Do this** — the concrete tasks, each with a kind tag
  (<span class="task-kind task-craft">craft</span>,
  <span class="task-kind task-retrieve">retrieve</span>,
  <span class="task-kind task-place">place</span> …),
  what it **needs** (inputs) and what it **yields** (outputs);
- **Rewards** (and **Costs**, for exchanges);
- a collapsible **Background** with the real-world context.

## Item chips

Items appear as chips: :ci[rope] is an item, and :ci[plant_fibers|10] is an item
with a count (here, ten). The little square is the item’s icon — or a
category-tinted monogram until its art is finished.

## Station slots

<span class="slot in">in ×N</span> input slots ·
<span class="slot fuel">fuel ×N</span> fuel slots ·
<span class="slot out">out ×N</span> output slots ·
<span class="slot none">no panel</span> works just by being placed.

## Tier colours

Each tier owns an accent colour, used on its chapter page, rungs, and cards:
Survival (moss), Stone (ash), Metal Ages (copper), Steam (brass),
Steel & DC (gunmetal/amber), Electric (arc-blue), Solid State (violet),
Space (indigo).
