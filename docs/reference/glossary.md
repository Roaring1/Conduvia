# Real-process glossary

Conduvia’s crafting tree is a tour of how humanity actually makes things. Here’s
the real process behind each major leap on the climb — the “why this is real” in
one place. Simplifications are noted; the chemistry is honest.

!!! note inline end "Honest by design"
    Quantities and times are compressed for play. The *mechanisms* are real.

## Heat & carbon

**Charcoal (pyrolysis).** Heating wood without enough air drives off volatiles
and leaves nearly pure carbon. Charcoal burns hotter and cleaner than wood — the
first fuel good enough to smelt metal.

**Coke.** The same trick applied to coal: bake coal in the absence of air to
expel tar and gas, leaving porous, high-carbon coke that burns hot enough and
stays strong enough to support a blast-furnace burden.

**Quicklime.** Roast limestone (calcium carbonate) and it releases CO₂, leaving
quicklime (calcium oxide) — a flux for smelting and the basis of mortar.

## Metals

**Bronze.** Smelt copper, then alloy it with tin. The alloy is harder and melts
lower than pure copper — good enough to define an age.

**Wrought iron (bloomery).** A bloomery reduces iron ore at a temperature below
iron’s melting point, producing a spongy “bloom” of iron and slag you hammer out.
Low carbon, tough, forgeable.

**Steel (blast furnace + refining).** A blast furnace melts iron with coke and
flux into high-carbon pig iron; controlled removal of carbon gives steel — the
structural material of the industrial world.

## Power

**Steam.** Burn fuel to boil water; the expanding steam drives a piston or
turbine. Work that never tires — the engine of the first industrial revolution.

**DC dynamo → AC grid.** Spin a conductor in a magnetic field and you generate
current. A DC dynamo gets you started; three-phase AC lets you move power long
distances efficiently and run big motors — the gateway to industrial chemistry.

## Industrial chemistry (the Electric tier)

**Hall–Héroult (aluminium).** Dissolve alumina in molten cryolite and run a huge
current through it; aluminium metal collects at the cathode. Aluminium is
basically *bottled electricity* — impossible before the grid.

**Chlor-alkali.** Electrolyse brine (salt water) to get chlorine, hydrogen, and
sodium hydroxide — three feedstocks the rest of chemistry leans on.

**Ferroalloys & metallurgical silicon.** Carbothermic reduction in an electric
arc furnace: roast quartz with carbon to get ~98% silicon, the starting point
for everything solid-state.

## Semiconductors (the Solid State tier)

**Trichlorosilane → polysilicon (Siemens process).** Convert crude silicon to a
volatile chloride, purify it by distillation, then deposit ultra-pure
polysilicon — purity measured in parts per billion.

**Czochralski wafer.** Dip a seed crystal into molten silicon and slowly pull it
while rotating; a single perfect crystal grows. Slice it into wafers, and you’re
ready to print circuits.

## Space (the win)

**Cryogenic air separation.** Compress, cool, and distil air to separate liquid
oxygen and nitrogen. LOX is your oxidiser.

**Kroll process (titanium).** Reduce titanium tetrachloride with magnesium to get
titanium sponge — light, strong, heat-tolerant.

**Superalloys (Inconel) & Cu-Cr.** Nickel superalloys for turbopumps, copper-
chromium for regeneratively-cooled chamber walls. Now you can build an engine
that survives its own exhaust — bolt on Al-Li tanks and RP-1, stack a stage, and
launch.
