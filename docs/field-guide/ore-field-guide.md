## How to Use This Guide
Found something you don't recognise? Pick a category on the left and match what you see. Every entry has an 'In Conduvia' section describing exactly what it looks like in-game.

## Salt Flat & Crystal Deposits
White crusts, salt pans, and layered crystal beds in dry basins. Halite, gypsum, sylvite.

## Metal Ore Veins
Brassy, metallic, or dark minerals in quartz veins. Green staining = copper nearby. Rusty cap = look below. Chalcopyrite, galena, sphalerite, pyrite.

## Iron Ores & Bulk Rock
The big feedstocks: iron for steel, limestone for cement, silica for glass, bauxite for aluminium. Usually massive, not small veins. Hematite, magnetite, bauxite, limestone, silica.

## Surface & River Finds
Materials you can grab with no mining at all: riverbed pebbles, bog nodules, surface float. Flint, obsidian, native copper, bog iron, cassiterite.

## Deep & Instrument Finds
Late-game materials you won't identify by eye alone. Use your scanner. Spodumene (lithium), rare earth ore, uraninite.

## Halite (Rock Salt)
Food seasoning; chemical feedstock (chlorine/caustic soda); de-icing.

## Gypsum (Calcium Sulfate)
Plaster and drywall; cement additive; soil conditioner.

## Sylvite (Potash Ore)
Fertilizer potassium source; potassium chemical feedstock.

## Chalcopyrite (Copper Iron Sulfide)
Copper ore -- primary source of copper for wiring, electronics, and alloys.

## Galena (Lead Sulfide)
Lead ore (batteries, shielding); often carries significant silver value.

## Sphalerite (Zinc Sulfide)
Zinc ore -- galvanizing, alloys and brass, batteries; may carry minor cadmium and indium.

## Pyrite (Iron Sulfide -- Fool's Gold)
Not a modern iron ore; historically used for sulfur and sparks; a major indicator mineral for nearby metal systems.

## Hematite (Iron Oxide)
Primary iron ore for steel; also a red pigment (ochre).

## Magnetite (Iron Oxide)
Iron ore; also produces natural magnetism (the basis of historic lodestone compasses).

## Bauxite (Aluminum Ore)
Primary aluminum ore (alumina to aluminum); can carry gallium as a byproduct.

## Limestone (Calcium Carbonate Rock)
Steelmaking flux; cement and concrete backbone; soil pH neutralizer; lime source.

## Quartz / Silica (Sand or Quartzite)
Glass and foundry sand; ceramics; silicon pipeline starter; quartzite as a high-grade silica source.

## Flint (Chert)
Stone Age toolmaking material; fire-starting sparks; sharp edges from conchoidal fracture.

## Obsidian (Volcanic Glass)
Ultra-sharp blade material; volcanic glass formed by rapid cooling of silica-rich lava.

## Native Copper (Elemental Copper)
The earliest copper route -- cold-workable metal nuggets and masses; later smelted.

## Bog Iron (Limonite)
Early iron source from wetlands; bloomery-friendly; renewable on long timescales.

## Cassiterite (Tin Oxide)
The primary tin ore; essential for bronze alloy. Found as heavy dark pebbles in stream gravels.

## Spodumene (Lithium Ore)
Lithium for advanced batteries; also used in ceramics and glass inputs.

## Rare Earth Ore (e.g. Bastnäsite)
Rare earth elements for magnets, lasers, and electronics; a critical late-tech supply chain.

## Uraninite (Pitchblende)
Uranium ore for nuclear fuel and late-game power chains; requires careful handling.
