    Tier 4 — Steel & DC¶

    The blast furnace and the first electricity. Steel structure, then a dynamo to spin it all.

    Tier 4 — Steel & DC

    Briefing

    From Steam Ironworks to Steel & Power¶

    From Steam Ironworks to Steel & Power¶

    Your steam plant has proven the principle: controlled heat, moving pressure, turning shafts.

    Tier 3 asks you to scale all of it.

    • Blast furnace replaces your bloomery and wrought-iron forge with coke-fired pig iron production
    • Bessemer and open-hearth converters turn pig iron into steel -- harder, tougher, weldable
    • Rolled products become structural material for bridges, ships, and boiler shells
    • High-pressure boilers push your engines far beyond Tier 2 limits
    • Compound engines do more work with less coal
    • Dynamos are steam turned to electricity
    • DC networks wire your site for the first time
    • Motors put power exactly where you need it, not just where a shaft happens to run

    This is the tier where your works stop being a collection of machines and start being a site.

    Win-path

    Coke Blast Furnace Stack¶

    Unlocks after: From Steam Ironworks to Steel & Power

    Coke blast furnace¶

    Your bloomery reduced iron without melting it. This is different.

    A coke blast furnace runs hot enough to produce liquid pig iron -- iron with 3–4% dissolved carbon, poured from a tap hole at the base. It's not wrought iron. It's brittle cast metal that feeds the converters.

    The hot-blast stove preheats the incoming air, cutting coke consumption by 30–40% compared to cold-blast operation. It's one of the most important efficiency upgrades in 19th-century metallurgy.

    Load ore, coke, and limestone flux in alternating layers from the top. Tap pig iron from the bottom. The furnace runs continuously -- never cools.

    Tip · Plan ahead: this chapter needs ~200+ pig iron total. This quest proves the furnace works (40 pig iron). The Bessemer quest that follows consumes 80, and the open-hearth quest consumes another 80. Keep your blast furnace running between quests and build a stockpile before you start converting.

    Do this: - retrieve Have or place the blast furnace and hot-blast stove.
    yields: SBsmall blast furnace×1 HBhot-blast stove×1 - retrieve Line the furnace with refractory bricks.
    yields: FRfireclay refractory brick×40 SRsilica refractory brick×40 - retrieve Feed crushed ore, coke and flux into the blast furnace.
    yields: CHcrushed hematite×60 CFcoke fuel×40 LFlimestone flux×20 - retrieve Tap pig iron from the blast furnace to prove it works.
    yields: PIpig iron ingot×40

    Rewards: PIpig iron ingot×20 CFcoke fuel×20

    Bessemer: Fast Bulk Steel¶

    Unlocks after: Coke Blast Furnace Stack

    Bessemer converter: fast bulk steel¶

    Pig iron is brittle because it contains too much carbon. The Bessemer process fixes this in minutes, not hours.

    Load the converter with molten pig iron. Tilt it upright. Blast cold air through the tuyeres at the base. The air burns out excess carbon -- the heat is generated by the carbon combustion, not from external fuel. The heat is self-sustaining.

    In 15–20 minutes: bulk steel.

    Bessemer is fast and cheap. It's not precise -- composition control is poor. Use it for rails and structural shapes where exact carbon content matters less.

    Tip · Pig iron demand for this chapter is high. This quest alone consumes 80 pig iron, and the open-hearth furnace quest that follows consumes another 80. Before you start, make sure your blast furnace can keep up -- tap and stockpile at least 200 pig iron before you begin converting. Falling short mid-quest is frustrating.

    Do this: - retrieve Have or place a basic Bessemer converter.
    yields: BCBessemer converter×1 - retrieve Charge the converter with pig iron from your blast furnace.
    yields: PIpig iron ingot×80 - retrieve Produce steel ingots from the Bessemer blow.
    yields: SIsteel ingot×40

    Rewards: SIsteel ingot×20

    Siemens–Martin Open-Hearth¶

    Unlocks after: Bessemer: Fast Bulk Steel

    Siemens–Martin open-hearth furnace¶

    Bessemer is fast. Open-hearth is careful.

    The furnace uses regenerators -- alternating chambers of heated brickwork that pre-heat combustion air by recycling exhaust heat. This lets the furnace reach temperatures high enough to fully melt the charge rather than just blowing air through it.

    Full melting means you can add scrap, control the slag chemistry, and sample the heat repeatedly before tapping. The result: better composition control and higher-quality steel for pressure vessels and load-bearing structures.

    Slower than Bessemer, but the furnace that built bridges, battleships, and boilers.

    Do this: - retrieve Have or place an open-hearth furnace.
    yields: OHopen-hearth furnace×1 - retrieve Charge the open-hearth furnace with pig iron.
    yields: PIpig iron ingot×80 - retrieve Produce refined steel ingots from the open-hearth melt.
    yields: SIsteel ingot×60

    Rewards: SIsteel ingot×20

    Rails, Beams and Boiler Plate¶

    Unlocks after: Siemens–Martin Open-Hearth

    Rails, beams, and boiler plate¶

    Steel is only useful when it can be shaped into precise structural forms. A rolling mill squeezes hot steel between shaped rollers to produce uniform cross-sections: rails, I-beams, angles, flat plate.

    Rolling replaces forging for bulk production. The shapes are consistent. The tolerances are tight enough to bolt together.

    What this unlocks: - Steel rails: your transport network scales with them - Steel beams: buildings and bridges that don't rely on timber or cast iron - Boiler plate: steel pressure vessels for the next stage of engine upgrades - Gusset plates: structural joints that actually hold under dynamic load

    The rolling mill is one of the loudest, most impressive machines in the works. It should look like it.

    Do this: - retrieve Have or place a steam rolling mill.
    yields: RMrolling mill (steam)×1 - retrieve Stock rolled products (rails, beams, plate, gussets).
    yields: SRsteel rail×40 RSrolled steel beam×16 RSrolled steel plate×20 SGsteel gusset plate×12

    Rewards: RSrolled steel beam×4 RSrolled steel plate×6

    Steel Boilers, Higher Pressure¶

    Unlocks after: Rails, Beams and Boiler Plate

    Steel boilers: higher pressure¶

    Your Tier 2 boilers ran on wrought iron plate at moderate pressure. That's a hard ceiling -- wrought iron fatigues at high pressure cycles.

    Steel plate is stronger, more consistent, and can be rolled thicker. Riveted steel shells with proper stays can safely run at 2–3× the pressure your old boilers could handle.

    Higher pressure means the same mass of steam does more work per stroke. Your engines are about to get significantly more powerful for the same fuel.

    The weighted safety valve isn't cosmetic. Overpressure kills people and destroys sites.

    Do this: - retrieve Have or place the riveted boiler shells.
    yields: RBriveted boiler shell×2 - retrieve Stock plate, lagging, pipe, and safety valves for the rebuild.
    yields: RSrolled steel plate×20 BLboiler lagging×10 WIwrought-iron steam pipe×12 WSweighted safety valve×2

    Rewards: WIwrought-iron steam pipe×6 WSweighted safety valve×1

    Compound & Mill Engines¶

    Unlocks after: Steel Boilers, Higher Pressure

    Compound engines: do more with the same steam¶

    A simple engine uses steam once, then exhausts it at near-boiler pressure -- wasting most of its energy.

    A compound engine uses steam in stages: a high-pressure cylinder takes the initial expansion, then exhausts into a larger low-pressure cylinder for a second expansion. Same boiler. Same coal. Significantly more shaft power.

    The Corliss valve gear adds variable cut-off: steam admission stops partway through the stroke, letting expansion do more work before exhaust. This is the engine that made 19th-century mills competitive.

    The flyball governor keeps shaft speed stable under varying load -- important for workshops, rolling mills, and eventually dynamos.

    Do this: - retrieve Have the required build items ready.
    yields: SBsimple beam engine×2 FGflyball governor×2 WIwrought-iron line shaft×4 MSmachine shop (steam)×1

    Rewards: FGflyball governor×1 LFleather flat belt×4

    Steam-Driven Dynamo House¶

    Unlocks after: Compound & Mill Engines

    Steam-driven dynamo house¶

    Your engines turn shafts. A dynamo turns a shaft into direct current.

    The principle: coils of wire rotating in a magnetic field induce voltage. Early dynamos use wrought iron cores for the field magnets and laminated armature cores to reduce eddy current losses.

    Belt-couple a reliable engine to two dynamos in a dedicated bay. Wire the outputs to copper bus bars at the switchboard. The machine shop now has electrical capability.

    This is the moment your site produces electricity. Everything that follows -- lights, motors, instruments -- is downstream of this.

    Do this: - retrieve Have or place the dynamos and electric machine shop bay.
    yields: DDDC dynamo (workshop)×2 MSmachine shop (electric)×1 - retrieve Stock magnet cores, armatures, and bus bars for the dynamo house.
    yields: WIwrought-iron magnet core×4 LAlaminated armature core×4 CBcopper bus bar×8

    Rewards: CBcopper bus bar×4 ICinsulated copper wire×20

    Low-Voltage DC Network & Lights¶

    Unlocks after: Steam-Driven Dynamo House

    Low-voltage DC network and lights¶

    Electricity is only useful when it goes somewhere.

    Run insulated copper wire from your dynamo switchboard to lamp fixtures across the works. The circuit is simple: dynamo → switchboard → fuse → switch → lamp. The voltage drop over distance limits how far you can push a DC network -- this is not a long-distance transmission system, it's a local lighting and small-power grid.

    The incandescent lamps use carbon filaments (1879-era technology). They're fragile, short-lived, and dim by later standards. But they are electric light. Your site can now operate after dark.

    The wooden switchboard, knife switches, and rewireable fuses are early electrical safety discipline. They are not decorative.

    Do this: - retrieve Have or place the switchboard panel and lamp fixtures.
    yields: WSwooden switchboard panel×1 ILindustrial lamp fixture×20 - retrieve Stock switches, fuses, insulators, wire, and lamps.
    yields: HKheavy knife switch×4 RFrewireable fuse×8 PPporcelain pin insulator×20 ICinsulated copper wire×80 ILincandescent lamp (carbon)×20

    Rewards: ICinsulated copper wire×40 RFrewireable fuse×8

    Motors on the Shop Floor¶

    Unlocks after: Low-Voltage DC Network & Lights

    Motors on the shop floor¶

    A DC motor is a dynamo run in reverse: send current through it and the shaft turns.

    The first practical DC motors were installed in factories in the 1880s. They did not immediately replace line shafting -- the transition was decades-long and gradual. But they changed what was possible:

    • A machine tool could now run independently of the main shaft
    • A hoist could be placed exactly where lifting was needed
    • A department could be stopped for maintenance without halting the whole plant

    Install DC motors on at least four key tools or hoists. Keep some shafting -- the transition is real, not instant. The remaining shafting will disappear in Tier 4 and beyond.

    Do this: - retrieve Have the required build items ready.
    yields: SDsmall DC motor×4 WIwrought-iron line shaft×2 MSmachine shop (electric)×1

    Rewards: SDsmall DC motor×2

    Tier 3 -- Steel & DC Age Online¶

    Unlocks after: Motors on the Shop Floor

    Tier 3 online: steel and DC power¶

    Your site is no longer a steam mill with iron parts.

    It is a steel plant with electric light.

    Confirm what's running: - Coke blast furnace producing pig iron continuously - Bessemer converter for bulk steel heats - Open-hearth furnace for controlled, high-quality steel - Rolling mill producing rails, beams, and boiler plate - High-pressure riveted steel boilers - Compound or mill engines with governors on the main shafts - DC dynamo house with a working switchboard - DC distribution network with lighting and fuses - DC motors installed on key machines and hoists

    This is the mature 1880s–1890s heavy industrial site. Tier 4 adds AC, transformers, induction motors, vacuum tube electronics, and the first centralised control systems.

    Rewards: SCSteelworker's Crate×1
