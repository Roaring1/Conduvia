    Stations¶

    Stations are the machines and work surfaces you craft at. Each one is modeled on a piece of real-world equipment. Drop a station from your inventory to place it, then open it to queue recipes. Stations marked passive have no crafting panel — they work or monitor automatically once placed.

    AC District Network
    network_ac_district
    in ×1 out ×2

    Gathers the power from several generators and spreads it across a whole area — a hub where the wires meet before fanning out to the buildings around it.

    Can't be opened — it just shows the network's state.

    AC Grid Mimic Panel
    panel_mimic_ac_grid
    in ×1 out ×2

    A board that shows your whole power grid at a glance — how much each generator is making, which switches are on or off, and where something has gone wrong. (A status board like this is called a mimic panel.)

    Just for reading — there's nothing to click.

    AC Induction Motor
    motor_ac_induction_small
    in ×1 out ×2

    Plug it into back-and-forth electricity and it just spins — no contacts rubbing or wearing out, so it's tough and nearly maintenance-free. (Back-and-forth current is called AC, and this kind of motor is an induction motor.)

    Needs an AC supply at the right frequency.

    in ×1 out ×1

    A gas engine bolted to an alternator. Load raw natural gas into the input slot and it burns it to put AC onto the line on demand, venting CO2 to the output. It only supplies power while it is running and fuelled -- your dispatchable backup for when the wind drops and the sun is down.

    Load natural gas to run; it feeds AC to the wired network.

    Hot Blast Stove
    hot_blast_stove
    in ×1 out ×2

    Burns the blast furnace's own waste gas to heat a tall stack of firebrick red-hot. Then the draft is switched: cold air is blown through the hot brick and comes out scorching. Feeding the furnace hot air instead of cold lets it run far hotter on much less fuel. This isn't a crafting station -- it's a helper that makes the furnace next to it work better. (It stores heat in brick and hands it back to the air -- a regenerative, or 'Cowper', stove.)

    Place it next to a Blast Furnace to preheat its air blast.
